Paratha

Description

Paratha is a type of Indian flatbread that is soft, flaky, and often layered. It is typically made from whole wheat flour (atta) and cooked on a tawa (griddle) with ghee or oil. Parathas can be plain or stuffed with various fillings like potatoes (aloo paratha), paneer (paneer paratha), radish (mooli paratha), or lentils (dal paratha). They are usually served with yogurt, pickles, chutney, or curries and are popular for breakfast or as a hearty meal. The texture can range from crispy to soft, depending on the cooking method and amount of fat used.


Ingredients

  • Wheat flour
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Ghee
  • Potato(Optional)

Steps

  1. Add water to flour
  2. Prepare the dough
  3. Take a part of the dough
  4. Flatten the part
  5. Put on pan
  6. Apply ghee
  7. Cook till golden brown
